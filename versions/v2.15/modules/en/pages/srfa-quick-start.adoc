[#_prerequisites]
== Prerequisites

Before starting, ensure you have the following:

* An AWS account with billing enabled.
* IAM permissions to create roles, or access to AWS CloudFormation.
* A modern web browser.
* An {rancher-product-name} (SRFA) account credential.

[#_5_step_quick_start]
== 5-Step Quick Start

[#_step_1_log_in]
=== Step 1: Log In

. Navigate to your {rancher-product-name} URL.
. Log in with your credentials.
. The {rancher-product-name} home page appears with quick action cards.

[#_step_2_link_aws_account]
=== Step 2: Link AWS Account

Connect your AWS account to {rancher-product-name}:

. Click the *Link AWS Account* card.
. Provide the following details:
** *Name*: `my-aws-prod` (or any unique name)
** *Default Region*: Select your AWS region (for example, `us-east-1`)
. Select *I need to set up a new role*.
. Click *Launch Stack*. This opens AWS CloudFormation in a new tab.
. In CloudFormation:
** Check the acknowledgment box.
** Click *Create stack*.
** Wait for the `CREATE_COMPLETE` status.
. Go to the *Outputs* tab.
. Copy the *RoleArn* using the format `arn:aws:iam::<account-id>:role/<role-name>` (for example, `arn:aws:iam::123456789012:role/SRFAManagementRole`).
. Return to the {rancher-product-name} tab.
. Click *I've Created the IAM Role*.
. Paste the Role ARN and click *Create*.

[NOTE]
====
For alternative methods, refer to the xref:rancher-admin/srfa-credentials.adoc[AWS Credentials guide] if you cannot use CloudFormation.
====

[#_step_3_create_eks_cluster]
=== Step 3: Create EKS Cluster

Create your first cluster using the wizard:

. Click *Create EKS Cluster* from the home page.
. In the *Cluster Setup* step:
** *Name*: `my-first-cluster` (must be unique).
** *Region*: Select the same region as your credential (for example, `us-east-1`).
** *Cloud Credential*: Select the credential created in Step 2.
** Click *Next*.
. In the *Node Configuration* step:
** Select a preset that fits your needs, such as the *Small (S) - Development* preset for testing, or a larger option for production environments.
** Click *Next*.
. In the *Review* step:
** Verify the configuration and estimated costs.
** Click *Create*.

The provisioning progress page appears.

[TIP]
====
You can safely close your browser; provisioning continues in the background.
====

Provisioning phases include:

Phase 1:: EKS Control Plane
Phase 2:: Node Groups
Phase 3:: Rancher Agent
Phase 4:: Agent Connection

[#_step_4_wait_for_completion]
=== Step 4: Wait for Completion

Monitor the provisioning progress:

* Checkmarks appear as each phase completes.
* Real-time status updates show the current operation.
* A success message displays when the EKS cluster is ready.

[#_step_5_deploy_an_application]
=== Step 5: Deploy an Application

When the cluster is ready, deploy an application:

. Click *Deploy Applications* on the success banner, or navigate to *Cluster Manager* > *Explore* > *Apps* > *Charts*.
. Search for `nginx`.
. Click the nginx chart and select *Install*.
. Configure the following:
** *Name*: `my-nginx`
** *Namespace*: `default`
** Keep the default values for the remaining options.
. Click *Install*.
. View the workload by navigating to *Workload* > *Deployments* > `my-nginx`.
